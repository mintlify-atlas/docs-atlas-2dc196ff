TDengine is an open source time-series database purpose-built for Industrial IoT, connected vehicles, and large-scale monitoring. It ingests, stores, and analyzes petabytes of time-series data per day generated by billions of sensors — with industry-leading performance and a built-in AI agent for forecasting and anomaly detection.

## What makes TDengine different

TDengine's unique "one table per device" design combined with supertables for cross-device aggregation delivers performance that consistently outpaces competing time-series databases.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="10x Faster Ingestion" icon="bolt">
    Outperforms InfluxDB and TimescaleDB by up to 10.6x on write throughput with columnar storage and lock-free writes
  </Card>
  <Card title="Built-in AI Agent" icon="brain">
    TDgpt connects to time-series foundation models for forecasting, anomaly detection, and imputation — no external tools needed
  </Card>
  <Card title="Simplified Stack" icon="layer-group">
    Stream processing, caching, and data subscription are built in — eliminating Kafka, Redis, and Flink from your architecture
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deploy TDengine">
    Choose your deployment method: [Docker](/get-started/docker), [installation package](/get-started/package), or [TDengine Cloud](/get-started/cloud).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Understand the data model">
    Learn about [tables, supertables, metrics, and tags](/concepts/data-model) — the foundation of TDengine's performance advantage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write and query data">
    [Insert time-series data](/concepts/insert) using SQL or schemaless protocols, then [query it](/concepts/query) with time-series SQL extensions like `INTERVAL()` and `FILL()`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your application">
    Use one of the [client libraries](/reference/connectors/overview) for C/C++, Python, Java, Go, Rust, or Node.js — or connect via the [REST API](/reference/connectors/rest-api).
  </Step>
</Steps>
